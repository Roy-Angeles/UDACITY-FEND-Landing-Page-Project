Venues

Stadium 974

Seating Capacity: 40,000

Designed by: Fenwick Iribarren Architects

Qatar 2022: Seven matches up to round of 16 stage

The stadium is mainly made of shipping containers and will be entirely demounted upon completion of the World Cup.

Education City Stadium

Seating Capacity: 40,000

Designed by: Fenwick Iribarren Architects

Qatar 2022: Eight matches up to quarter-finals stage

The facade features triangular patterns, a reimagination of traditional arabesque patterns, that appear to change color during the day following the sun's movement across the sky.

Ahmad bin Ali Stadium

Seating Capacity: 40,000

Designed by: BDP Pattern, Ramboll, and AECOM

Qatar 2022: Seven matches up to round of 16 stage

The stadium's main design features is the undulating facade, made of patterns that characterize different aspects of the country, such as "the importance of family, the beauty of the desert, the native flora and fauna, as well as local and international trade.

Khalifa International Stadium

Seating Capacity: 40,000

Designed by: Aspire Zone Foundation

Qatar 2022: Eight matches up to the third-place play-off

The stadium boasts an ultramodern shape and dual arches that represent continuity and embracing fans from all over the globe. Inside the stadium, all seating areas are protected from the elements by a canopy, with further climate control delivered by advanced cooling technologies.

Al Janoub Stadium

Seating Capacity: 40,000

Designed by: Zaha Hadid Architects + AECOM

Qatar 2022: Seven matches up to round of 16 stage

The design is inspired by the city's history of pearl diving, fishing, and traditional dhow boats.

Lusail Stadium

Seating Capacity: 80,000

Designed by: Foster + Partners and Populous

Qatar 2022: Ten matches including the final

The design is inspired by the interplay of light and shadow seen in the Fanar lantern. The structural form and façade echo the intricate motifs of art and furniture pieces found across the Arab and Islamic world during early civilizations.

Al Bayt Stadium

Seating Capacity: 60,000

Designed by: AS+P Albert Speer + Partner and Dar Al-Handasah

Qatar 2022: Nine matches up to semi-finals stage

The stadium's design is similar to nomad's tents, the upper tier of the modular design seating is portable, and will be removed following the championship, and given to developing nations in need of sporting infrastructure.

Al Thumama Stadium

Seating Capacity: 40,000

Designed by: Ibrahim Jaidah Architects & Engineers

Qatar 2022: Eight matches up to quarter-finals stage

The stadium's design weaves Arab culture through its facade, drawing inspiration from the woven patterns of a Gahfiya, the traditional head piece worn by Muslim men in the Arabian region.
